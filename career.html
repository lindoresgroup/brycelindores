Career

Cycling achievements:

  • 2006 Queensland Tandem Cyclist of the Year.
  • 2006 Cycling World Championships – BRONZE.
  • 2008 Beijing Paralympic Games – BRONZE in the Men’s Individual Pursuit B VI 1-3 event. * 2009 Climbed Mount Kilimanjaro.
  • Bryce was part of a 25-member-team to complete the High Sight Expedition, to reach the summit of Kilimanjaro, Uhuru Peak, Highest Mountain in Africa. The group broke a record of having seven non-sighted climbers at the summit.
  • 2010 Road World Cup – GOLD.
  • 2011 World Track Championships – BRONZE 4km individual pursuit.
  • 2011 Cycling World Championships – GOLD.
  • 2011 National Championships – GOLD in road time trial and SILVER in the road race.
  • 2012 World Track Championships – GOLD in tandem 4km pursuit.
  • 2012 Queensland Tandem Cyclist of the Year.

Teamwork

Bryce’s pilot, Mark Jamieson, has recently relocated from Tasmania to concentrate on training with Bryce full time ahead of the 2012 London Paralympic Games.
Mark, who competed as an able-bodied racer in Beijing, says good communication skills are essential for the pair to efficiently work as a team.

“Communication is the key. If Bryce or I am having a bad day, then it shows. We have to peddle at the same time and work as a team, so the communication element is very important,” Mark says.
Bryce says trust is another important factor, with the pair reaching speeds of up to 110km/h. The pair met after a triathlon in Noosa, Qld. Shortly after, the opportunity came up for Mark to join Bryce when Mark became eligible (to be eligible, you have to have been out of the international circuit for two years, and three years after an Olympics).
“We did the nationals in December 2011, won the nationals, and almost got the world record in our first race together”.
“Riding tandem is quite a different skill,” Mark says. “You have to be pretty talkative and similar body sizes and similar physical capabilities”.

Training:

Bryce and pilot Mark train together on a tandem bike full time, clocking up around 800km road kilometres per week.
They also train at the gym three times a week to work on core strength and stretching exercises which are critical in tandem cycling.

Funding:

There are numerous costs associated with cycling and competing on the world stage. According to Bryce, the Queensland Academy of Sport provides some assistance in the form

of equipment including wheels and handlebars, however the rest is funded by himself and his pilot.
The tandem bikes cost in the region of $15,000 and usually require replacement every 18months.

The transportation of the cycling equipment can also be a costly exercise – with excess luggage fees totalling thousands of dollars.
Bryce would be very interested in considering any suitable sponsorship opportunities.

Inspirations:

While Bryce considers various sports-people and professionals as inspirational, he himself happens to be an inspiration to all he meets.

His inspirations include fellow cyclists Robbie McEwen and Nick Gates, as well as former Wallabies captain John Eales and former world motorcycle champion Troy Bayliss.

Future:

Bryce’s main focus is on the upcoming 2012 London Paralympic Games.
He and pilot Mark are currently putting all of their efforts into their gruelling training regime. In May/June, the pair will head off to Europe to compete in two World Cups and two tour events. They will visit Belgium, Italy, Spain and France.
In September the pair will compete at the 2012 Paralympic Games in London where they are considered to be hot favourites.
Bryce, who has spoken/presented at various events and functions, is keen to take on additional public speaking engagements to engage and inspire.
